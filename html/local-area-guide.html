  • Alcatraz

    This infamous rock is a must see. There are day and night tours available; both sell out super fast, so book your tickets in advance. The view of the city from the island sometimes triggers a vague sense of pity for the prisoners who were once there. It can be cold and dark, even during the daytime on this island, so keep your jackets on.

    Learn More »

  • Cable Cars

    Every person who visits San Francisco has a picture of one of these iconic landmarks. It’s epic and for a reason. For $6, you can go up and down the hills of the City, catching some amazing views in the process. The ride starts from Market Street and goes all the way down to the Ghirardelli Square, which is perfect to continue the sightseeing at the Fisherman’s Wharf.

    Learn More »

  • Fisherman's Wharf

    Clam Chowder, crab stands and San Francisco souvenirs. Fisherman’s Wharf is best known for its daily fresh seafood. It’s very close to Pier39, another tourist haven. While you take a stroll along the bay and feed sea gulls, enjoy a live performance by some street artists or get a nice surprise from the infamous bush man.

    Learn More »

  • Ghirardelli Square

    San Francisco’s very own chocolate factory where you get to play Willy Wonka. If you are a chocolate fiend and want to indulge in one of their massive sundaes or shakes, step in. You have nothing to lose as you get a free chocolate anyway, plus you get to see their chocolate making process while sipping on that sin.

    Learn More »

  • Golden Gate Bridge

    Apart from being a landmark for the foggy city and one of the most photographed bridges in the world, this is one sight you don’t want to miss. If you find yourself on a rare, clear day in San Francisco, don’t miss the famous views from the Vista Point and Hawk Hill. The view from the other side of the bridge allows you to fully appreciate the majestic San Francisco skyline.

    Learn More »

  • Golden Gate Park

    Golden Gate Park is the third most visited park in the United States. While the park is free to visit during the day, popular attractions charge admission, such as deYoung Museum, California Academy of Sciences and Conservatory of Flowers. The park is filled with gardens, museums, art, flowers, trees, lakes, birds and wildlife.

    Learn More »

  • Pier 39

    Pier 39 is a 45-acre waterfront complex that is a gathering place for millions of San Francisco locals and visitors. In addition to its 14 full-service restaurants, 90+ shops and popular attractions, PIER 39 is home to a 5-acre waterfront park and a 300-berth marina. PIER 39 is known for its spectacular views of San Francisco Bay including the Golden Gate Bridge, Bay Bridge and Alcatraz, as well as the world famous California sea lions hauled out on K-Dock.

    Learn More »

  • Twin Peaks

    One never gets tired of all the views San Francisco has to offer—there are many hidden locations and secret spots. Twin Peaks are two large hills near the geographic center of the City. Except for Mount Davidson, they are the highest points in the city. The view from the lookout point offers a panoramic view the whole city, and is something that should not be missed.

    Learn More »

  • Union Square

    San Francisco's own Union Square is full of character and ives Manhattan’s Union Square a run for its money. This area is surrounded by high-end retail shops and department stores, making it an epicenter for shopping. Just like the varied spirit of San Francisco, Union Square gets a new theme almost every month, be it ice rinks during the holidays or acting as home base for the Nike Marathon.

    Learn More »
